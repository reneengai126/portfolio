Dataset Introduction

County Health Rankings & Roadmaps (CHR&R), a program of the University of Wisconsin Population Health Institute, releases annual datasets that include county-level variables related to health disparities that draws attention to why there are differences in health within and across communities. The most recent data from 2024 contains most up-to-date county health and demographic measures.

We have a pair of datasets: county_health_data.csv and county_demographic_data.csv - The former dataset relates to health factors, including variables such as adult smoking rates, physical inactivity, alcohol impaired driving deaths, and poor mental health days. - The latter relates to demographic factors, including variables such as high school graduation rate, school segregation, voter turnout, and homicides.

# Write your dplyr code here to manipulate the data (Step 2)

new_data<-joined_county_data%>%
   drop_na(Median.Household.Income.raw.value, Poor.Mental.Health.Days.raw.value, State.Abbreviation.x)%>%
  group_by(Median.Household.Income.raw.value, Poor.Mental.Health.Days.raw.value)%>%
  arrange(Median.Household.Income.raw.value,Poor.Mental.Health.Days.raw.value)%>%
  filter(Median.Household.Income.raw.value>15000)%>%
  filter(Poor.Mental.Health.Days.raw.value>6)%>%
  select(High.School.Graduation.raw.value, Median.Household.Income.raw.value, Poor.Mental.Health.Days.raw.value, State.Abbreviation.x, Disconnected.Youth.raw.value)%>%
  top_n(50, High.School.Graduation.raw.value)
  

# Write your ggplot code here to answer the research question (Step 3)

ggplot(new_data, aes(x=Median.Household.Income.raw.value, y=Poor.Mental.Health.Days.raw.value))+
  geom_point(aes(color=State.Abbreviation.x))+
  geom_smooth()+
  geom_text(aes(label=State.Abbreviation.x), size=1.5)+
  labs(x="Medium Household Income",y="Poor Mental Health Days",title="Figure 1: Relationship between Median Household Income and Poor Mental Health Days")+
  theme_bw()
